At Your Disposal

It's easy to get lost during an extended chat with an LLM.

As the interaction spirals out in different directions, we become disoriented. This is frustrating and wasteful of time, effort and resources.

One response is to augment the LLM by increasing its context window.

The alternative is to bolster the human. We can do this by structuring and documenting human-LLM interactions.

We have chosen the metaphor of Ariadne's Thread to describe our approach to solving this problem. Ariadne handed the Greek hero Theseus a ball of thread before he entered the labyrinth at Knossos so that he could retrace his steps after confronting, and killing, the Minotaur.

Escaping the labyrinth

A thread is a collection of snapshots, or representations, of a chat. It summarises decisions, ideas, requirements, dependencies, risks, etc. up to that point.

It is both a summary of exploratory design steps and a way to track changes.

It allows us to explore the labyrinth without getting lost.

By way of example, we have created developer inputs and LLM outputs suitable for iterating over designs for a new project via prototypes (conceptual, vertical, functional, etc.).

Turning our design chat into a prompt thread

To turn a chat into a prompt thread we pass in a thread definition (what we want to do, and how we want to do it).

Our default template is for a prototype thread. It has two parts:

  • Prompt thread definition:- properties specific to a prototype thread
    • Title, description, definition, prototype type, etc.
  • LLM instructions:- common to all threads
    • Interaction guidelines
    • Terms
    • Snapshot schema

A snapshot is a summary of the chat to date. It is what an LLM returns to us.

A collection of snapshots is a prompt thread.

The project README has a complete guide to creating a prompt thread plus tips and best practices.

Prompt thread workflow

A thread definition prompts us to consider what we want to gain from a chat.

At any point during the chat, we can ask the LLM to generate a snapshot. Do this when you detect a discernible shift in thinking or approach, or when you want to review what you have done.

Snapshots have several functions:

  • Capture progress (decisions made, etc.)
  • Allow us to step back and review progress while we consider what to do next
  • Collectively act as a record of a chat (a thread)
  • Break down unwieldy chats into manageable parts
  • Encourage us to penetrate further into the labyrinth

Conclusion

LLMs are excellent at small, discrete tasks. They are typically reliable at convergent thinking (refining ideas) and with a little oversight can help speed things along.

They also expand our ambitions by plugging gaps (handling unfamiliar languages or APIs) and doing heavy lifting (writing tests, generating diagrams).

They are endlessly patient but they are not cheap. Their cost will, at best, be deferred and it will be paid in finite resources such as water, pollution and emissions as well as money.

Ariadne's Thread

Ariadne's Thread emerged from two different but complementary wishes.

  • To be independent of LLMs (because of their cost)
  • To know where our chats are taking us (so as to not get lost)

Dependency avoidance

We use LLMs (quite a lot) but we are not reliant on them. Where possible we create something new from our interactions, something which does not require their continued presence. We think LLMs are valuable but not indispensable.

The work on Ariadne's Thread is itself an example of this principle in practice; we worked with three LLMs to test its viability but we haven't made the solution dependent on having access to an LLM.

Critical thinking

LLMs are less good at divergent thinking (creation of novel ideas) than they are at convergent thinking but they can still be of help.

Transforming a chat into a loosely structured prompt thread means we can be more ambitious in our thinking.

But in order not to perish in the labyrinth, we must first take hold of the thread of our reasoning, and hold to it fast.

Terms

Thread
The thread serves as a breadcrumb trail through the development process, allowing both human developers and LLMs to retain context and coherence across multiple interactions. The developer maintains a record of the thread in a thread history.
Snapshot
Each addition to the thread. A specific interaction state representative of progress to date.
Prompt thread
A series of prompts connected by a thread. To convert a chat into a prompt thread pass in a thread definition and/or a thread or snapshot.
