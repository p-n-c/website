At Your Disposal

It's easy to get lost during an extended chat with an LLM.

As the interaction metastasises, the developer becomes disoriented. This is frustrating and wasteful of time, effort and resources.

One approach is to increase the context window thereby enabling a richer and more specific experience or use multiple LLMs, or both.

There is another, less impactful way. More important than the prompts the developer puts to the LLM, are the human prompts they direct towards themselves.

Escaping the labyrinth

There is no evidence of spontaneous intelligence in LLMs. The developer is solely responsible for what happens, and makes all of the decisions. There is therefore an extreme asymmetry between the human and the LLM when they work together.

If the developer is director, writer and conductor, they must know what's going on; they need a goal or a plan, a way to oversee what is happening, and a means of getting back on track when the discussion deviates.

Convergent / divergent thinking

The interactions described here relate in the main to convergent cognitive tasks which are generally more goal-oriented and structured.

When it comes to divergent thinking, the developer is advised to be sceptical of LLM responses and to not become attached to their outpourings.

Whatever the task, use LLMs sparingly.

While LLMs may provide short-term boosts in creativity during assisted tasks, they might inadvertently hinder independent creative performance when users are asked to perform without assistance. This raises important questions about the long-term impact of repeated LLM use on human creativity and cognition.

Human Creativity in the Age of LLMs by Kumar et al.

Ariadne's Thread

Ariadne's Thread fulfills these requirements by using prompt threads.

They are a consistent, informal approach to structuring and documenting interactions.

Setting up a prompt thread

A prompt thread mediates the interactions between a developer and an LLM during the conversion of an idea into a prototype.

At the outset of a discussion on prototyping a new idea, the developer submits two files:

  • A Project Prototype Definition (PDD)
  • A thread schema

Given a PDD, the developer and the LLM start their back-and-forth. When a significant juncture is reached, the developer asks the LLM to generate a thread extension which is a snapshot of progress to date but which continues, or extends, the thread of the discussion.

The snapshot is disposable and standalone but the developer has the option to create a thread history.

A single snapshot, or the entire thread history, are available to instigate an interaction with another LLM or another developer.

Each snapshot also marks a pause in - or an end to - the interaction, a moment for the developer to collect their thoughts, review the decisions they have made, and consider the responses they have received before moving on.

Critical thinking

There is debate as to whether LLMs promote or hinder critical thinking and human creativity.

In her paper Generative AI’s Impact on Critical Thinking: Revisiting Bloom’s Taxonomy Chahna Gonsalves frames a study of MSc Marketing students using a modified version Bloom's Taxonomy (a framework for categorising educational goals) to assess their use of LLMs during assignments.

In the discussion, Gonsalves warns

Although AI can promote critical thinking, it can also undermine autonomous cognitive development if students bypass deeper engagement, highlighting the importance of fostering critical engagement strategies in AI-driven education.

Generative AI’s Impact on Critical Thinking: Revisiting Bloom’s Taxonomy by Chahna Gonsalves

LLMs are tools. Use them wisely. Don't become dependent, and remember they come at a cost we may not be able to afford.

Conclusion

There are short-term benefits from using LLMs but it remains true that developers, and others, put at risk their ability to think critically and creatively when they cede control and direction of their projects and ideas to LLMs.

LLMs are at their best when we concentrate on the questions we ask ourselves. Ariadne's Thread is designed to focus on these human prompts, and the oversight we need when interacting with LLMs.

LLMs are design grid lines or guides; helpful tools that can be removed when the work is done.

The question we ask ourselves is not can I do more, but I can I do more of what is interesting or novel.

Generative AI can unlock unprecedented creative growth and learning, allowing users to expand their cognitive horizons and engage in innovative forms of thought.

Human Creativity in the Age of LLMs by Kumar et al.

Terms

Thread
The thread serves as a breadcrumb trail through the development process, allowing both human developers and LLMs to retain context and coherence across multiple interactions. The developer maintains a record of the thread in a thread history.
Thread extension
Each addition to the thread. An extension can be thought of as a snapshot - a specific interaction state representative of progress to date.
Prompt thread
A prompt that has been designated a thread by passing in a PPD.
