How web development teams adapt to AI

Towards a new paradigm

LLM roles: practical as a planning tool and destination as interface

The Universal Interface

The state of development today

  • The cost of web app and page development is high
  • Interfaces must adapt to a variety of devices and visitors
  • Visitors, supported by LLMs that are capable of interacting with APIs, will be able to create interfaces relevant to user or visitor needs and wants thereby bypassing or reducing the role of development teams.

Contract as a multi-purpose generative artifact.

The value of APIs

In the interim, and possibly long term, it will not be possible - or desirable - for LLMs to interact directly with databases or have knowledge of their internal schema.

APIs provide a controlled and safe way access to data whilst preserving company privacy and honouring data protection issues.

Traditional APIs may be augmented to include pricing, design and ethics.

Existing teams

  • create an API if there isn't one
  • internal benefits
    • source of truth for frontend/backend contracts
    • source of truth for (front and backend) tests
  • ready for 'competing' interfaces including those created by LLMs

New teams

New teams can adopt new structures and roles.

  • traditional API plus add-ons - may not require an API schema; internal and external APIs may only require endpoints and examples - can be used to create db, act as source of truth in contract first scenarios and testing

Downsides

  • maintenance
  • loss of detail
  • black box/es
  • loss of jobs
  • loss of knowledge and broader understanding
  • generic approaches
  • loss of 'new' ideas and approaches; evolutionary stagnation

Upsides (if prepared)

  • preparing for inevitable change (even though nature of change uncertain)
  • retain knowledge by adaptation creating teams that are better e.g. more
  • fit for modern websites and apps better communication between (the former roles of) design, development, UX, etc. more interesting roles better than superannuated approaches wrapped in AI opportunity to change
